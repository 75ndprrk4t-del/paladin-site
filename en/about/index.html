About PALADIN

Project Overview

PALADIN (Powering Adults for Literacy And Digital Inclusion in Navigating financial risks) is an Erasmus+ Cooperation Partnership in Adult Education. Our project addresses the critical need for digital and financial inclusion among Baby Boomers and Generation X.

Why PALADIN?

As physical bank branches decrease, digital services are becoming the primary option. Many adults face a choice: endure the risks of digital fraud or face exclusion from the financial world. PALADIN provides a third way: empowerment through education.

What We Do

  • Tailored Curriculum: We develop modular courses (equivalent to 9 ECTS) specifically designed for the needs of older learners.
  • Blended Learning: Our concept combines flexible online materials with face-to-face support and local learning centers.
  • Open Access: All our results and materials are provided as Open Educational Resources (OER) to ensure widespread impact across Europe.

Our Partnership

The project brings together a diverse consortium of universities and social organizations from five European countries:

  • Germany: University of Bamberg (Lead) & Caritas Association for the Archdiocese of Bamberg
  • Slovenia: Simbioza Genesis
  • Hungary: Andrássy University Budapest
  • Spain: Ayuntamiento de Benetússer
  • Iceland: Reykjavik University

Project Goals

By 2028, we aim to have established a robust European network and trained hundreds of participants, fostering a more resilient and digitally inclusive society.
